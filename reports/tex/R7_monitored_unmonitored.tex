\begin{abstract}
Report R5 established that the directed transition graph $M$ is not a lossy
picture of a dissipative rule's channel but the \emph{exact} dynamics of a
different experiment --- the \emph{monitored} QCA, in which every qubit is
measured each cycle --- and that for $92$ of the $240$ \rt{pbc} rules the
\emph{unmonitored} channel $\Phi$ has an attractor that no set of
computational-basis states can name. This report closes that gap constructively.
We introduce the doubled-space \emph{pair graph} $\GG$, whose diagonal is exactly
$M$ and whose cyclic structure entrywise bounds the support of every peripheral
eigenoperator of $\Phi$ (Theorem~3). This yields, per $(\text{rule},N)$, a
rigorous \emph{certificate} (Theorem~4): when it fires, the Tier-1a attractor
data is exact for the unmonitored channel. We validate the whole construction
against the dense $4^N$ superoperator --- the certificate is \emph{sound} on all
$480$ dissipative units at $N=4$ (never declaring exactness where coherence
exists) and the support bound holds for every peripheral eigenoperator --- and
report the certificate coverage over the sweep. The pair graph also exposes a
class of conservation law invisible to $M$: diagonal \emph{weak charges}, which
grade the protected coherence of the wall-Hadamard core (rules
$22,23,146,151,178$), resolving an open item of R5. Two corrections to the naive
picture are worth stating up front. First (erratum), graph-transient population
does \emph{not} generally decay under $\Phi$: rule $22$ keeps
$\approx 12.5\%$ of its weight on states the monitored graph calls transient. The
correct invariant is sector confinement (Theorem~1), not transience.
Second, ``Hadamard-free $\Rightarrow$ classical'' is too coarse: a \rt{V}-free
rule with a limit cycle of period $\ge 2$ carries a \emph{protected rotating
coherence} between the synchronised configurations, and correctly fails the
certificate; only rules whose cycle labels de-synchronise (the additive rules
$90/165$) dephase it.
\end{abstract}

\section{Setup and the two experiments}
A dissipative rule's one-cycle channel is
$\Phi(\rho)=\sum_b K_b\,\rho\,K_b^\dagger$, with the global Kraus operators $K_b$
indexed by the frozenset $b$ of sites at which the jump branch $A_1$ fired in the
fixed even-then-odd brickwork order (R6, \fn{core/cycle.py}). Two experiments
share these Kraus operators:
\begin{itemize}
  \item the \textbf{monitored} QCA measures every qubit in the computational
  basis each cycle. Its dynamics is the classical Markov chain
  $M_{yx}=\sum_b|\bra{y}K_b\ket{x}|^2$ --- exactly the Tier-1a transition graph.
  \item the \textbf{unmonitored} channel is $\Phi$ itself. Coherences feed back
  into populations, so $\Phi$ is \emph{not} a function of $M$.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Erratum to the design notes.} An early design note asserted that
population on monitored-transient states decays under $\Phi$. This is false in
general. For rule $22$ ($\rt{IVVD}$) at $N=4$ the stationary states of $\Phi$
have $\approx 12.5\%$ of their weight on states that are transient for $M$; the
attractor support is an $11$-state set that strictly contains the $3$ monitored
pointer states. What actually holds is sector confinement, Theorem~1: a state may
be transient for $M$ yet immortal for $\Phi$, provided it lies in the same
weakly connected component as a surviving coherence. This is why the pair graph
is built on the full sector block, not on the recurrent states alone.

\section{Definitions}
Write $d=2^N$. Operators act on $\mathbb{C}^{d}$; superoperators on
$\mathrm{End}(\mathbb{C}^d)$, which we coordinatise by pairs $(x,y)$ of basis
states, $A=\sum_{x,y}A_{xy}\ket{x}\bra{y}$.
\begin{definition}[fixed-point data]
$\Fix(\Phi)=\{A:\Phi(A)=A\}$ is the fixed-point \emph{operator} space; its
dimension is the \emph{geometric} multiplicity of eigenvalue $1$, computed by
SVD (R6, \fn{quantum/peripheral.py}). A \emph{peripheral} eigenoperator has
$|\lambda|=1$. The C\'esaro projection
$\overline{\Phi}=\lim_{T}\frac1T\sum_{t<T}\Phi^t$ exists, is itself CPTP, kills
every $|\lambda|<1$ and every peripheral phase, and has range $\Fix(\Phi)$; its
rank is the \emph{within-sector} $\dim\Fix$ we use below.
\end{definition}
Three attractor sizes are compared throughout:
\[
  K_M := \#\{\text{terminal SCCs of }M\}\ \le\
  \dim\Fix(\Phi)\ \le\ |\Prec|,
\]
with $\Prec$ defined in \S3. $K_M$ is a purely monitored (classical) count;
$\dim\Fix$ is the channel's exact fixed-point dimension; $|\Prec|$ is the pair
graph's combinatorial upper bound.

\section{The pair graph and the theorems}
\begin{definition}[pair graph $\GG$]
Nodes are ordered pairs $(x,y)$, encoded as $(x\ll N)\mid y$. There is an edge
$(x,y)\to(x',y')$ iff some Kraus label $b$ has both $\bra{x'}K_b\ket{x}\neq 0$
and $\bra{y'}K_b\ket{y}\neq 0$. Equivalently, joining the labelled single-cycle
branches of $x$ and of $y$ on \emph{identical} labels,
$\mathrm{succ}_2(x,y)=\bigcup_{b}\supp(K_b\ket{x})\times\supp(K_b\ket{y})$.
\end{definition}
The label join is exactly the same-$b$ requirement of $\sum_b K_b(\cdot)K_b^\dagger$;
joining across labels would fabricate cross-branch interference, and skipping the
join would lose edges. Every matrix element is computed in the exact ring
$\mathbb{Z}[1/\sqrt2]$, so a zero created by intra-branch interference deletes the
edge exactly.

\begin{proposition}[structure]\label{prop:struct}
(i) The diagonal subgraph $\{(x,x)\}$ of $\GG$ is exactly $M$.
(ii) Swap symmetry: $(x,y)\to(x',y')$ iff $(y,x)\to(y',x')$.
(iii) Confinement: every $\GG$-edge stays inside
$\mathrm{WCC}(x)\times\mathrm{WCC}(y)$, where WCC denotes a weakly connected
component of $M$.
\end{proposition}
Parts (i)--(ii) are checked as runtime asserts (R6 tests); (iii) is Theorem~1.

\begin{theorem}[sector confinement; sectors are unmonitored-exact]\label{thm:t1}
For a set $S$ of basis states the following are equivalent: (a) $S$ is closed
under $M$-edges; (b) $\operatorname{span}(S)$ is invariant under every Kraus
operator $K_b$; (c) $\operatorname{span}(S)$ is an enclosure of $\Phi$. Hence the
WCC partition is an \emph{exact} invariant decomposition of the unmonitored
channel, and $\Phi$ is block-diagonal across the induced operator blocks
$S_i\otimes S_j^{*}$. What does \emph{not} transfer is the recurrent/transient
split and the SCC refinement inside a WCC: those are monitored-relative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
(a)$\Leftrightarrow$(b): $M_{yx}\neq0$ iff some $K_b$ has
$\bra{y}K_b\ket{x}\neq0$; closure of $S$ under all such $y$ is exactly invariance
of $\operatorname{span}(S)$ under each $K_b$ (a one-step, interference-immune
property). (b)$\Rightarrow$(c): if each $K_b$ preserves $\operatorname{span}(S)$
then so does $\Phi$, and by trace preservation no weight leaks out. Block-diagonality
follows because $K_b\ket{x}\bra{y}K_b^\dagger$ keeps the left index in
$\mathrm{WCC}(x)$ and the right in $\mathrm{WCC}(y)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[lower bound]\label{thm:t2}
$K_M \le \dim\Fix(\Phi)$: each terminal SCC carries at least its own stationary
state.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[upper bound]\label{thm:t3}
Let $\Prec := \mathrm{FwdCl}_{\GG}(\text{cyclic pairs of }\GG)$, where a
\emph{cyclic pair} lies in a $\GG$-SCC of size $>1$ or carries a self-loop. Then
every peripheral eigenoperator $A$ of $\Phi$ (in particular every fixed point)
has $\supp(A)\subseteq\Prec$. Consequently
$\dim\Fix(\Phi)\le|\Prec|$, and any protected coherence lives on the
off-diagonal part of $\Prec$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
For $|\lambda|=1$, $A=\lambda^{-t}\Phi^t(A)$ for all $t\ge 0$, so
$\supp(A)$ is invariant under the support map of $\Phi$, which is precisely
forward propagation along $\GG$. A pair in $\supp(A)$ therefore admits
arbitrarily long in-paths within $\supp(A)$; by finiteness (pigeonhole) it is
reachable from a $\GG$-cycle, i.e.\ it lies in $\mathrm{FwdCl}$ of the cyclic
pairs.
\end{proof}
The forward-closure form is the price of a purely combinatorial bound: it cannot
detect amplitude cancellation, so $\Prec$ can strictly overcount. It is,
however, rigorous, and it is what makes the certificate below sound.

\begin{theorem}[exactness certificate]\label{thm:t4}
If the off-diagonal part of $\Prec$ is empty and its diagonal part equals the
$M$-recurrent set, then every peripheral eigenoperator of $\Phi$ (restricted to
each sector) is basis-diagonal and supported on the recurrent classes. In that
case the Tier-1a attractor data $(K_M,\text{sizes})$ is \emph{exact} for the
unmonitored channel; we record \fn{certified}$=$\rt{true}.
\end{theorem}

\paragraph{What the certificate does \emph{not} claim.} It is a per-sector
(diagonal-block) statement. Cross-sector coherences --- e.g.\ a stationary
$\ket{x}\bra{y}$ between two frozen states in \emph{different} sectors, which
inflate the full $\dim\Fix$ by up to $F^2$ for $F$ frozen states --- are the
``noiseless pairing'' content and are reported separately (the
\fn{--cross-sector} pass); they do not bear on the attractor \emph{structure}.
Accordingly we compare like-for-like: the sandwich uses the \emph{within-sector}
$\dim\Fix$ (sum of per-WCC C\'esaro ranks), for which
$K_M\le\dim\Fix_{\text{within}}\le|\Prec|$ holds exactly.

\paragraph{Computation.} Because a cyclic pair projects to an $M$-cycle on each
coordinate and terminal SCCs are $M$-closed, $\Prec$ is contained in
$\mathrm{Cyc}\times\mathrm{Cyc}$ (states on $M$-cycles) and its forward closure;
the pair graph is therefore run on the WCC-diagonal blocks only, streamed and
never materialised beyond the block. Fragmented rules have small WCCs, so $\GG$
stays cheap far beyond the dense-$\rho$ ceiling $N=8$ of the Tier-1b census;
near-ergodic rules are capped by a node budget and reported bound-only. (R6,
\fn{graph/pair\_graph.py}.)

\section{Validation}
The certificate and the bound were checked against the exact $4^N$ superoperator
(\fn{quantum/peripheral.py}) on \emph{all} $480$ dissipative $(\text{rule},bc)$
units at $N=4$ and a sample at $N=5$:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bound chain:} $K_M\le\dim\Fix\le|\Prec|$ holds on every unit.
  \item \textbf{Support containment (Thm~3):} every peripheral eigenoperator of
  the full superoperator has entrywise support inside the cross-sector $\Prec$
  --- $0$ violations.
  \item \textbf{Soundness (Thm~4):} no unit certifies \rt{true} while carrying
  within-sector coherence --- $0$ unsound certificates, checked against the
  WCC-restricted superoperator on all $240$ dissipative rules at $N=4$.
\end{itemize}
The certificate is \emph{conservative}: a combinatorial support bound cannot see
amplitude cancellation, so $89$ of $480$ genuinely diagonal units at $N=4$ fail
to certify. This is by design --- when the certificate declines we report the
sandwich interval, not a false negative.

\paragraph{A refinement of ``\rt{V}-free $\Rightarrow$ classical''.} It is
tempting to expect every Hadamard-free rule to certify. It does not, and the pair
graph explains why exactly. A \rt{V}-free rule is deterministic on basis states,
so a limit cycle of period $p\ge 2$ is a genuine classical cycle
$c_0\to c_1\to\cdots\to c_0$. The coherence $\ket{c_i}\bra{c_j}$ is a peripheral
eigenoperator \emph{iff} the Kraus labels are synchronised around the cycle. Rule
$10$ ($\rt{DEDD}$) has a $2$-cycle with matched labels and therefore a protected
$\lambda=\pm1$ coherence --- confirmed directly in the superoperator spectrum ---
so it correctly certifies \rt{false}. The additive rules $90/165$ ($\rt{DEED}$,
$\rt{EDDE}$) have longer cycles whose reset labels de-synchronise; the coherence
dephases and they certify \rt{true} despite having a cycle. Thus the sharp
criterion is label synchronisation, which $\GG$ computes, not the mere presence
of a cycle.

\section{The visibility theorem and weak charges}
\begin{theorem}[visibility]\label{thm:vis}
Diagonal \emph{strong} charges $s:\text{basis}\to\mathbb{Q}$ --- conserved along
every single-branch $M$-edge, equivalently constant on WCCs --- are exactly the
diagonal part of the strong commutant $\{K_b\}'$; their number is the diagonal
dimension of that commutant. Diagonal \emph{weak} charges impose \emph{no}
constraint on $M$ (a diagonal unitary acts trivially on diagonal states); they
are precisely the $\GG$ difference-conservation laws
$s(x)-s(y)=\text{const}$ along every $\GG$-edge $(x,y)\to(x',y')$. Non-diagonal
symmetries of either kind are visible only at operator level (Tier 1b / Tier 2).
\end{theorem}
We detect both with a range-$r$ local-density ansatz with even/odd sublattice
split, solving the linear systems exactly over $\mathbb{Q}$ (R6,
\fn{quantum/weak\_charges.py}). The charge spaces nest $C\subseteq S\subseteq Z$
(constants $\subseteq$ strong $\subseteq$ $\GG$-conserved), giving certified
integer counts $n_{\text{strong}}=\dim S-\dim C$ and, restricting the difference
law to the off-diagonal part of $\Prec$ (so that structurally-decaying
coherences do not contribute vacuous laws), a count of weak charges that
\emph{grade} the protected coherence.

\paragraph{Result (R5 open item i).} The protected coherence of the
wall-Hadamard core is graded by a single weak charge, invisible to the monitored
dynamics, with a small integer $D$-spectrum:

\begin{center}\small

W & tuple & \#strong & \#weak & grades coherence ($D$-values) \\
\midrule
$22$ & \rt{IVVD} & $0$ & $1$ & yes $\{-2,\dots,2\}$ \\

$146$ & \rt{DVVI} & $0$ & $1$ & yes $\{-2,\dots,2\}$ \\

\end{center}
The graded quantity is a domain-wall--like density: the weak charge partitions
the off-diagonal support of $\Prec$ into $\Delta$-sectors, exactly the structure
a monitored observer cannot see.

\paragraph{One-line remark on the classical family.} For a \rt{V}-free rule the
Kraus operators are partial permutations, so the monitored and unmonitored
\emph{populations} coincide; the nontrivial content is entirely the coherence
sharpness among the \rt{V}-carrying rules, and --- as \S4 shows --- even the
classical family is not uniformly certifiable once limit cycles appear.

\section{Outlook}
The pair graph is the $p\to\infty$ end (measure every cycle is $M$; never measure
is $\Phi$) of a measurement-every-$p$-cycles family; the intermediate Zeno axis
connects to measurement-induced phase transitions. The weak-charge candidates and
the diagonal/off-diagonal $\Prec$ structure are the natural handoff to a Tier-2
commutant analysis: a weak charge is a diagonal element of the bilocal commutant
that the monitored chain cannot see, and its $\Delta$-grading of $\Prec$ is a
concrete decomposition for the operator-level symmetry search.
